New Editorial Direction

PMC Content on CAP.org

Heretofore, content on CAP.org focused on...

  • Curation and lists
  • High-level descriptions
  • Repurposing extant content
  • This often sends end users to other websites

Burning Question: What content can the PMC offer our end users (ie, readership) that they can't already find elsewhere?

What can the PMC offer w/r/t content that our users will not be able to find elsewhere? This brings me to my proposed editorial direction for new content on the site moving forward.

New Focus

  • Narrowed scope
  • Long-form content
  • CAP-specific expertise

The PMC is uniquely positioned to create focused content on practice management, pathology, and how both relate to membership with the CAP.

Why a new editorial direction?

Search Engine Optimization*

  • Keep users on the site
  • Establish credibility
  • Focus on engaging long-form content
  • Take a "web first" approach
* This is a fancy way of saying that we want people to find our content through Google.

How will we support the new editorial direction?

  • Dedicated in-house staff
  • New editorial process
